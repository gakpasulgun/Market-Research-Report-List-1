<p><p>Water supply management involves the strategic allocation and distribution of water resources, categorized primarily into surface water and groundwater markets. Surface water refers to water bodies like rivers, lakes, and reservoirs, which are generally easier to access but can be subject to seasonal variations and pollution. Groundwater, sourced from aquifers beneath the earth's surface, is often more stable but requires careful extraction to prevent depletion or contamination. Both markets focus on sustainable usage and equitable distribution to meet community needs.</p></p>
