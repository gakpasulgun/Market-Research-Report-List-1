<p><p>Non-superconducting Fault Current Limiters (NSFCLs) are devices used to protect electrical systems from fault currents by limiting their magnitude. The market for NSFCLs is primarily divided into two types: saturable core and solid-state. Saturable core NSFCLs utilize magnetic materials that become saturated under high current, effectively reducing fault current levels. In contrast, solid-state NSFCLs employ semiconductor technology to manage and limit fault currents more rapidly and with greater precision, offering advantages in control and response time.</p></p>
