<p><p>Airflow sensors for medical devices are categorized into amplified and unamplified types. Amplified airflow sensors include built-in electronics that enhance the output signal, making them suitable for applications requiring high sensitivity and precision. These sensors provide robust performance in detecting even subtle changes in airflow. In contrast, unamplified airflow sensors generate a raw output signal that requires external amplification for processing. While they may be more cost-effective, they typically demand additional circuit components for signal interpretation, making them less ideal for sensitive medical applications.</p></p>
