<p><p>The modular ice makers market is primarily divided into water-cooled and air-cooled ice makers. Water-cooled ice makers utilize water for cooling, offering efficient performance, especially in high-capacity settings, but require a continuous water supply. In contrast, air-cooled ice makers rely on ambient air for cooling, making them more versatile and easier to install, but they may be less efficient in extremely warm environments. Both types cater to various commercial applications, balancing efficiency, installation requirements, and operational costs.</p></p>
